Club History

Bass Rocks Golf Club was founded in 1896 and golf was first played by members in the area now known as "The Meadows," our current 15th through 18th holes. At the time this was a six hole course designed by the Scottish golfer, Alex Findlay. Over the years multiple expansions occurred at the Club, most notably the 1913 expansion to 18 holes by the golf course designer H.C. Leeds (architect of Myopia Hunt Club).

Robert McNeil has been working with the Club for many years, recently presenting a guiding master plan of improvements to the membership in 2024. Scroll through the image gallery to the right to learn more about the master plan and the completed phases of the project.
